## Anti-Patterns Verdict

The comp does not look like generic AI output: there are no gradients, glass cards, decorative hero metrics, or gratuitous chrome. It does still read more like a polished terminal specimen than a complete fantasy workflow. Initials-only identity, a detached receipt card, and inert controls keep it below Sleeper/DynastyNerds/KTC category parity.

The deterministic detector reported one advisory `numbered-section-markers` hit at line 0. It is a false positive caused by dates/frame numbering. Axe found serious definition-list semantics and builder-scaffold contrast failures; the product content itself had no contrast failure.

## Overall Impression

Daily Open has a strong morning answer, honest freshness, and disciplined lane color. The frozen state fails because v3's pixel changes drifted from the ratified v3.14 contract, and because the mobile/inspection affordances are drawings rather than usable compositions.

## What's Working

- Extractor counts, ranks, market labels, deltas, group census, movement order, and timestamps reconcile with the live 2026-07-11 artifact.
- Macro-first Daily Open and the neutral degraded banner answer the manager's morning question without false freshness.
- Blue/model and amber/market stay isolated; within-band states avoid verdict color and visible magnitude.

## Priority Issues

### [P1] Contract semantics drifted during the pixel pass

Movement copy uses `moved 4 pts toward ...` rather than lead-bound `DG/market lead grew/shrank 4pp`; board rows replace the signed display delta with a direction chip; tie cardinalities disappear; no-read lexicon regresses. These were explicit v3.14 RED seeds. Restore the contract, or route the material visual-direction change to David before changing the source contract.

### [P1] Mobile is neither the promised two-line row nor a resilient viewport

At 390px, mover and board rows wrap into three visual lines. Fixed 390px phone width plus page padding overflows a 390px viewport and clips at 320px/zoom. The bottom rail is normal flow and its span items are not keyboard-operable. Use responsive width constraints, fixed two-slot row geometry, and real sticky buttons/links.

### [P1] Accessibility omits the primary measure and interaction semantics

Mobile bar labels announce endpoints only, not the read plus stored gap. Kyle Williams loses the bar entirely. Tabs, search, and FA toggles are spans/divs rather than operable controls; the receipt `dl` is invalid. Build the accessibility tree as part of the composition, not after it.

### [P1] Free Agents nominates players and cites the wrong reason

`Closest calls, by disagreement: Keenan Allen and Kareem Hunt` is false as written: Waller and Ertz lead disagreement, while Allen/Hunt lead xVAR and are closest to replacement. Naming them in the headline also reads as a waiver recommendation. Keep the aggregate replacement truth, declare xVAR sort, and let rows carry identities.

### [P1] The complete inspect/degraded states are not composed

The degraded frames never demonstrate the required dashed market lane. The expanded receipt is detached below the table with no selected row, adjacent comparison, or Trade Lab staging action. Compose the full row-to-inspector workflow and the affected-lane failure state before build REDs.

### [P1] The evidence bundle does not satisfy the visual gate

`01-top.png` and `02-midscroll-daily-healthy.png` are byte-identical, so the mandatory healthy mid-scroll capture is absent. The independent seven-dimension mean is 7.0/10, with identity, density, mobile integrity, and benchmark parity all at 6.

## Persona Red Flags

**David, morning power user:** the headline says five real moves while the list implies fourteen; movement language mixes market-price direction with gap direction; inactive search/scope controls stop the drill-down.

**First-time dynasty manager:** identical native QB ranks can sit beside a 20-point gap, while widely separated ranks can say within band. The explanation lives behind a receipt, so the row initially looks contradictory.

**Keyboard/mobile user:** thumb-bar actions and segmented controls cannot receive focus, long rows wrap beyond the promised two slots, and the degraded mobile frame lacks a model/build timestamp.
