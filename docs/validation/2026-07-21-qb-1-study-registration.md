## 3. Target and label contract (D2)

- **Target `y(p,t)`:** regular-season Sleeper-scored fantasy points **per qualifying game** for player *p* in season *t*, under the §2.1 rule. **Postseason excluded everywhere.**
- **Qualifying game (exact):** a regular-season weekly row with `(attempts + sacks_suffered) ≥ 1 OR carries ≥ 1`. The games denominator is the count of qualifying games.
- **No dropback inclusion filter** — a minimum-volume filter would induce survivorship bias. A pre-registered **secondary** sensitivity slice at ≥4 qualifying games is reported alongside, and **never substituted for**, the unfiltered primary (seed F29).
- **Outcome classes (exhaustive, two-axis):** eligibility (`cohort_admitted` | `rookie_no_priors` | `cohort_ineligible_prior`) × target (`target_evaluable` | `no_target_season`), mapping to `OUTCOME_CLASSES = (evaluable, no_target_season, rookie_no_priors, cohort_ineligible_prior, cohort_ineligible_unobserved)`. `evaluable` = cohort-admitted with ≥1 qualifying game in *t*. Non-evaluable rows are **attrition-counted and reported as rates**, never silently dropped and never imputed to zero.
- **Fail-closed validation:** duplicate player-seasons, non-finite PPG, and missing games each refuse by name.

## 4. Cohort, rookie route, and folds

- **Cohort at fold *t*:** every QB with ≥1 career NFL dropback (`dropbacks_proxy`) through *t−1* **and** season *t−1* roster presence.
- **Roster-presence rule (pinned, deliberately most-inclusive):** a player is *t−1*-roster-present iff they have **≥1 roster row in season *t−1* with `game_type = REG` — any week, any status.** Chosen against survivorship bias: a midseason-cut QB with prior dropbacks is still a real prediction subject. The 2–8 QBs per season the boundary moves stay **IN**.
- **No age bound anywhere in eligibility.** An age cliff would be a binary predictive cutoff barred by the constitution's aging rulings, and would drop Brady's 41+ seasons from exactly the pocket-vs-rushing comparison the study exists to run. Age enters **only** as a continuous H4 feature (seed F12 guards 29/33/36/37/40 and any forced terminal zero).
- **Rookie route — registered as EXCLUSION, honestly:** a rookie = **no prior REG weekly row AND no prior REG roster row through *t−1*** (a *t−1*-rostered zero-stat QB is `cohort_ineligible_prior`, never a rookie). Rookies are **out of the veteran study**, classified `rookie_no_priors`. **QB-1 builds no rookie PPG predictor.** Consequence stated on every report: **the study speaks to veterans with ≥1 NFL season.** Daniels and Nix therefore enter the case panel as **second-season** predictions (2025 from 2024 features), and the panel says so explicitly.
- **Folds (pinned):** expanding window over target-season rows — train = target seasons `{2016..t−1}`, test = target season `t`, for **t ∈ {2018..2025} — 8 folds, 2024 included.** Rolling windows are **rejected and not revisitable**: N is too small to spend observations on window truncation; recency lives in regularization.
- **Earliest target season is 2016** (2015 is the earliest feature season).

- **H1–H4 pipeline:** train-fitted median imputer → train-fitted `StandardScaler` → **Ridge**, alpha selected **on train folds only** by leave-one-out/GCV from the fixed grid **{0.01, 0.1, 1, 10, 100}**. **No other estimator, scaling, or grid.**

## 7. Inference contract (pinned end to end)

- **Point estimate:** pooled paired delta = **evaluable-n-weighted mean** of per-fold paired deltas.
- **Interval:** **cluster bootstrap by player** (a resampled player carries all of their player-season rows), **10,000 resamples, RNG seed 20260716**; CI95 = **BCa**, bias correction from the bootstrap distribution, acceleration from the **delete-one-PLAYER jackknife** (jackknife unit = resampling unit).
- **p-value (BH-FDR input) — a real null, not a CI inversion:** paired **cluster permutation test**; under H0 each player cluster's lane assignment swaps with probability ½ per replicate; **p_two = (1 + #{|δ*| ≥ |δ_obs|}) / (B + 1)**, B = 10,000, seed 20260716.
- **Multiplicity:** **BH-FDR at q = 0.10** across the full primary set of 14. Everything else is labeled **exploratory**.
- **Materiality floor:** **0.05 Spearman units.**
- **Direction (model lanes):** every comparison registers its expected direction. `supported` requires pooled delta **in the registered direction** ≥ 0.05 **AND** CI95 excluding zero **AND** the fold floor **AND** surviving BH-FDR. A zero-excluding CI in the **wrong** direction is **`contradicted`** — named, never folded into "supported."
- **Fold floors:** model-lane comparisons need **≥5 of 8** evaluable folds; H5-lane comparisons need **≥3 of 4** DP folds. Below floor → **`unsupported_power`**, which is an honest outcome, not a failure.
- **Fold-level guards:** fold evaluable *n* ≥ 20 else **`fold_starved`** (no point estimate, CI-only display). Degenerate inputs (constant/all-null vectors, mass ties) emit named degenerate states; ties by average rank with tie counts reported.
- **Status vocabularies are disjoint by lane:** model-lane `{supported, not_separable, contradicted, unsupported_power}`; H5-lane `{market_noninferior, market_superior, model_superior, inconclusive, unsupported_power}`. **`supported` is impossible on an H5 contrast by construction.** The field is `support_status` — never `verdict`.

### 9.2 Inference contract for the H5 lane

*[Notation corrected at Codex review round 1, blocker B2: the margin and the boundary are distinct quantities and were previously conflated.]*

- **Effect definition (fixed once, used everywhere):** **δ = (market − model)** in Spearman units. A positive δ favours the market.
- **Margin and boundary are separate objects.** The **margin `m` = 0.05** is a **positive magnitude** (David-ratified 2026-07-21), symmetric with the model-lane materiality floor — one materiality scale across the study. The **non-inferiority boundary is the signed quantity δ₀ = −m = −0.05.** Wherever a boundary appears it is δ₀; wherever a magnitude threshold appears it is `m`.
- **Mandatory margin-sensitivity readout at m ∈ {0.025, 0.05, 0.10}** (boundaries −0.025 / −0.05 / −0.10), reported regardless of outcome, so the conclusion's margin-dependence is visible and never hidden.
- **The NI test:** the lane-swap permutation tests δ = 0 and is therefore **not** a valid test of the non-inferiority null. H5 primaries instead use a **null-centered (shifted) cluster bootstrap at the boundary**: `δ*_null := δ* − δ_obs + δ₀`, **p_NI = (1 + #{δ*_null ≥ δ_obs}) / (B + 1)**, same player-cluster resampling, B = 10,000, seed 20260716. This is a valid one-sided test of H0: δ ≤ δ₀ vs H1: δ > δ₀. **p_NI is the BH-FDR input** for H5 primaries; the reported interval is the **one-sided 95% lower bound** on δ.
- **Total ordered status function.** Exactly one `support_status` per H5 contrast, evaluated **strictly in this order, first match wins**:
  1. **`unsupported_power`** — the H5 fold floor (≥3 of 4) is unmet. **The power gate precedes every substantive label.**
  2. **`model_superior`** — the **two-sided BCa CI95 on δ excludes zero in the model's favour (δ < 0)** **AND** **|δ| ≥ m**. `p_perm` is reported **descriptively only**; **no FDR claim attaches to a superiority secondary**, and **no permutation-CI inversion exists or is used**.
  3. **`market_superior`** — the **two-sided BCa CI95 on δ excludes zero in the market's favour (δ > 0)** **AND** **|δ| ≥ m**. Same descriptive-`p_perm` rule.
  4. **`market_noninferior`** — **all three**: the fold floor **AND** the one-sided 95% lower bound of δ **> δ₀** **AND** BH-adjusted **p_NI ≤ q = 0.10**. **If the CI and p criteria disagree in EITHER direction (CI-pass/p-fail or CI-fail/p-pass), the status is `inconclusive` carrying the named `ci_p_disagreement` flag** — a registered rule, no discretion.
  5. **`inconclusive`** — everything else.
- **NI retention (the NI result is never lost to the single status):** every H5 contrast carries **`ni_met` (boolean) and `p_ni`** in the output alongside its `support_status`, so a non-inferiority finding remains visible even when a superiority state wins precedence.
- **`supported` is impossible on an H5 contrast by construction** — the two lane vocabularies are disjoint.
- **Rank-only.** DP values are consensus-scale, not PPG: Spearman/Kendall and top-k only; **RMSE/MAE are never computed on market values**; no PPG calibration is attempted.
- **Framing that must appear in every H5 artifact:** this tests the model against **expert consensus (FantasyPros ECR)**, **not** the trade market. A favourable result reads *"keeps up with expert rankings"* — **never** *"beats the market."* The trade-market question remains gated to Gate-4 (~Dec 2026). KTC is not touched.

### 9.3 Registered H5 gates and disclosures

- **Fold scope:** target seasons **{2021, 2022, 2023, 2024}**, fold floor **≥3 of 4**. Below floor → `unsupported_power`.
- **Paired population:** every model-vs-H5 comparison runs on the **exact common pool** (fold-evaluable ∩ DP-covered); common-pool *n* and per-lane coverage are reported separately.
