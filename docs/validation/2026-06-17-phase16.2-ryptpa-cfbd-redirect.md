## Why 16.2 Was Reopened, and the Falsification Frame

A prior bake-off (`phase16-4-wr-college-promotion-decision.md`, 2026-05-23) had already found RYPTPA and YPRR **NOT PROMOTED**, with a *structural* diagnosis ("coverage skews toward high-draft programs; signal may already be embedded in draft capital"). The cockpit re-scoped 16.2 away from a naive retry into a **pre-registered selection-bias falsification test**: the prior null ran at ~87.7% coverage, and the *missing* players were disproportionately the late-round / small-school edge gems (e.g., Puka Nacua, DeMario Douglas, Andrei Iosivas). Hypothesis: if the unresolved cohort is where RYPTPA edge lives, a governed ≥99%-coverage dataset that *includes* them could change the verdict.

**Pre-registered gate (cockpit-locked before any data was pulled):** the test is executable only if **≥90%** of the Day-3 (NFL rounds 4–7) / non-Power-5 WR cohort (2015–2024) has computable RYPTPA after a targeted CFBD fetch, with the residual being *identity*-blocked (Inc1-fixable), not *raw-data*-absent. Below 90% due to raw absence → REDIRECT.

## Method

- Read-only audit of the existing CFBD cache + the precedent feature pipeline (`scripts/build_w2b_cfbd.py`) and the cohort training file `app/data/training/prospects_with_outcomes_v3.csv`.
- Cohort: WR, draft years 2015–2024, Day-3 (rounds 4–7), non-Power-5 by college conference.
- Targeted minimal **live CFBD Tier-3 fetch** (David-authorized) of team season passing attempts (the RYPTPA denominator) for the cohort's missing school-years + the named examples.

---

## Finding: CFBD `/stats/season` Does Not Cover FCS

CFBD's `/stats/season` endpoint covers **FBS (including Group-of-5)** but **structurally does not cover FCS** team-season stats. Confirmed three independent ways:

1. The production-built tpa cache negative-cached `null` for every FCS school (the adapter tried and got empty).
2. A clean live test returned `[]` for `team=Princeton, year=2022` (Ivy/FCS).
3. **11 of the 13** Day-3/small-school cohort misses are FCS schools (Princeton, Pennsylvania, Monmouth, William & Mary, Central Arkansas ×2, Northern Iowa, Rhode Island, Samford, UT Martin, Connecticut), all returning empty; the **2 FBS** misses (Boise State, Southern Miss) have present denominators and are recoverable.

**Measured result:** post-fetch Day-3/small-school computable RYPTPA ≈ **32/43 ≈ 74%** — below the pre-registered **≥90%** gate. The shortfall is **raw denominator absence at the FCS level**, not identity resolution.

---

## Interpretation

The selection-bias hypothesis is **falsified at the data layer**: the FCS late-round edge gems (Iosivas/Princeton et al.) cannot be measured by CFBD at any access tier, so a governed Bronze→Silver→Gold pipeline would not recover the cohort that motivated the test. This sharpens the prior Phase 16.4 structural diagnosis with a precise mechanism (FCS unmeasurable in CFBD season stats).

An **FBS-only re-scope** was considered and rejected by the cockpit: it answers a weaker question, excludes the FCS gems that were the entire motivation, and does not overturn the Phase 16.4 null.

**Scope of what is falsified (precision):** RYPTPA *via CFBD* for the *FCS/late-round edge* population. This is a data-source limitation, not an eternal ban — a future FCS-capable source could warrant a fresh, separately-gated look. **`breakout_age`** remains a separable candidate but is untested here and would require its own data-completeness gate (and likely faces the same FCS wall, being CFBD-derived).
