## 0. Objective and charter trace

QB is the most valuable asset class in David's Superflex league and the weakest signal in both engines — a standing strategic mismatch (product report BUILD-4, P3). Engine A QB is grade D (negative R², 3 features, DEBT-3); Engine B `qb_v2` is ACTIVE_B but explicitly caveated (OOS R²=0.253, Spearman=0.711 — rank usable, magnitude weak). BUILD-4 gives QB dedicated treatment and absorbs the rookie-QB binary risk-filter (rookie fork, 2026-07-01). ## 1. The decision this serves (Gemini framing, 2026-07-03)

**The Rookie QB Risk-Insulation Line** — the central question of David's pick-hoard rebuild: *draft this rookie QB with hoarded picks, or trade the picks for an insulated veteran?* A missed QB pick resets a rebuild ~3 years; a hit locks a decade window. Secondary: *when has a veteran QB's team commitment degraded enough that liquidation-before-the-cliff is the honest read?* Everything in this spec traces to those two decisions. ## 3. Label design — the core reframe (three-way converged)

**Primary target: starter-regime retention, not points magnitude.** Both lanes independently converged: continuous magnitude is the hill both engines died on at QB n; dynasty QB value is dominated by the survival/regime question (does he keep a startable job), not the points question (how many, given that he does).

- **Label (v4 name honesty, Codex F7): `startable_role_occupancy@H`** for H ∈ {1, 2, 3} — binary per horizon. **Positive iff the player's season-T+H QB row shows games ≥ 8 AND mean snap share ≥ 0.50** — **the predicate is pinned as the FRACTION `snap_share >= 0.50`, never `>= 50`: nflreadpy `offense_pct` and the feature store are 0–1 scale, and the `>= 50` misread produces ZERO positives in every season (Codex F4, empirically probed). T1 RED seeds the 0.50-positive / 0.499-negative boundary.** This measures role occupancy (was he on the field as the starter), not fantasy efficiency. **Disclosed honestly (Codex F7 + Claude F3): the label conflates availability with job retention** — an entrenched starter with a week-3 season-ending injury at T+H labels negative; it is a role-occupancy/AVAILABILITY proxy, not pure team-commitment, and the validation report must bucket "established prior starters failing only via games<8" as label noise (injury-sensitivity annex). Real-QB replay (Gemini): Burrow-2020/Fields-21-23/Tua all positive; benched-2024-Fields negative; desperation-started Zach Wilson positive-with-terrible-features — the features, not the label, carry that separation, as designed. Rejected alternative (recorded): within-season ppg rank ≤ 24 (needs denominator/tie machinery; gameable by low-games efficiency spikes).
- **Pinned label mechanics (Codex R1 #3/#9):** one row per (player_id, season); duplicate source rows are deterministically REJECTED to quarantine, never a silent winner; games counts from the existing `games_t` construction (nunique stat weeks, `scripts/assemble_engine_b_dataset.py:165-173`; probe evidence max=21 implies postseason weeks are included — **T1's RED must assert the postseason-inclusion behavior before labels are computed**, since it moves the games≥8 boundary); **snap-share-missing-at-T+H fallback (pinned):** label by games ≥ 8 alone with a `label_basis="games_only"` disclosure column — source probe (Codex F6) shows the fallback is RARE from regenerated source (missing snap rows per season: 2018=3, 2019=6, 2020=3, 2021=5, 2022=10, 2023=1, 2024–25=0), and **T1 must report the games_only share by season/horizon and fail/disclose beyond a pinned tolerance (5% of labeled rows per season)** so a crosswalk regression can never silently make the fallback dominate; absent-row-at-T+H = negative, which conflates "<4 games" with "out of the league" — disclosed wherever the label definition surfaces. True games-started data does not exist in the repo; ingesting it is a §10 escalation, and upgrading the label is a named future decision, never a silent swap.
- **Class-balance note:** the §2 balance numbers (49/42/38%) were computed under the REJECTED rank proxy; T1 must recompute balance under the role-occupancy label and the §6 small-n gates apply to those recomputed counts.
- **Label regeneration task (scope pinned, Codex R1 #4):** T1 regenerates the per-horizon LABEL TABLE from the already-ingested nflreadpy source (same source, no new external source; PIT discipline: features strictly as-of season T, labels strictly from T+H). **Labels cannot close the 2024 feature-row hole:** candidate training rows remain the feature rows that exist (2018–2023); regenerating 2024 FEATURE rows is a separate scope item with its own tests, not silently included.
- **Relationship to continuous value:** survival does not replace xVAR — it **discounts** it (Gemini). The QB view composes P(startable@H) with the existing frozen magnitude estimate as a *descriptive overlay*; no composite score, no folding into Engine B mechanics, `decision_supported=false` recursively.
- Ordinal refinement (entrenched/fragile/non-starter) is a candidate SECOND increment after binary-per-horizon calibrates; not in scope for the first modeling pass.

## 6. Validation gates (pre-registered in the spec; R² demoted)

**Validation machinery (Codex R2 #2 — reuse honestly scoped):** the existing `WalkForwardDriver` is a Ridge REGRESSOR over `avg_ppg_t1_t2`; it is NOT directly reusable for per-horizon binary labels. BUILD-4 **reuses the expanding-window fold definitions and the temporal-isolation pattern** (train = feature_season < test_year; imputer/scaler fit on train only) but implements a **QB survival/classification walk-forward driver**. Initial model family (pinned so T3 is mechanical): regularized logistic regression as the baseline classifier (small-n-appropriate, natively calibratable); tree/boosted families are candidate extensions only if logistic is beaten on the pre-registered gates within the same driver. Gates per fold-horizon:
- **Calibration:** Brier score vs the base-rate benchmark PER HORIZON (beat the baseline or no promotion); reliability curve rendered in the validation report. **Baseline definition (pinned, Codex R1 #6):** the train-fold, per-horizon label prevalence applied as a constant probability to the test fold — fit on training labels only; a test-fold-prevalence baseline is a leakage defect (RED seed).
- **Discrimination:** ROC-AUC + top-k precision (k=12, the Superflex startable-half) per horizon.
- **Uncertainty:** BCa CIs on Brier/AUC deltas vs baseline; CI lower bound must clear zero for the promotion case.
- **R² on magnitude: secondary diagnostic only** — reported, never gating.
- **Leakage gates:** existing `validate_no_prohibited_features` + the NEW candidate-specific QB v3 validator (§5) on the candidate matrix per fold; the existing `validate_position_feature_contract("QB", …)` remains the untouched qb_v2 guard and must stay green separately; as-of audit on regenerated labels.
- **Small-n gates — no survivorship (Codex R1 #5, corrected by Claude F1 + Codex F5):** a fold-horizon with <30 evaluable rows or <10 minority-class rows emits `low_sample_qb_holdout` and is excluded from METRIC AVERAGING — but it still counts against promotion ELIGIBILITY. **Structural fold counts are per-horizon (Claude F1, Codex-verified): H1=4, H2=4, H3=3** (test-2023 H3 needs 2026 outcomes) — eligibility = **all-but-one of the horizon's STRUCTURAL folds evaluable** (H1/H2: ≥3 of 4; H3: ≥2 of 3), never a hardcoded "of 4". **Honest expectation set now (Codex F5, source-probed): under current class balance H1 likely fails the gates in 3 of 4 folds — H1 may be structurally NON-PROMOTABLE, and the validation report supports per-horizon non-promotion as an expected honest outcome, not a surprise failure.** The report lists every excluded fold-horizon explicitly. "Passes on the subset with enough data" is not a promotion case.

## 7. Abstention policy (first-class output, Gemini mandates)

The candidate emits per-player abstentions, not forced numbers. **Ordering is pinned (Codex R1 #7): abstention gates run BEFORE model scoring** — an abstained player gets NO candidate probability at all, only the disclosed prior/caveat; abstention is an eligibility mask applied to the cohort, not a post-hoc overlay on a computed score.
- **Day-3 rookies (picks 65+):** no model curve; base-rate survival prior + explicit "abstained — insufficient draft capital" disclosure. No sleeper-mining at n≈0.
- **Small-sample surgers:** a QB with games_t<8 at T and **no established prior season — pinned (Codex R2 #3): no T−1 feature row with games_t ≥ 8** — carries a `small_sample_qb` caveat; hot-backup efficiency does not create a starter runway. (Seed: a 7-game current season labels/abstains differently with vs without a qualifying T−1 row.) (Contract-status data is not in the repo; the contract-based cap Gemini proposed is carried as disclosure language, not a data field — §10.)
- **Off-season transfers/coaching changes:** quantitative outputs stay stable; the change surfaces as a qualitative regime caveat only (this is the existing constitution ruling restated for QB).
- **Unstable uncertainty: DEFERRED (Codex R1 #8).** Per-player CI-width abstention is a later increment — v1 pins no width number rather than inventing one; the deferral is explicit so RED authoring is mechanical without it.

## 8. Surfacing and the No-Verdict Line

Where it surfaces (later increment, after a validated candidate exists): survival **bands** ("3-year startable probability: 45–70%"), never tier labels ("Franchise QB", "Bridge"). **Baselines are self-computed, never folklore (Claude F2, Codex-confirmed):** every displayed historical baseline (rookie bust rate, cohort survival) is computed from OUR regenerated label table — segmented by draft-capital bucket per the fork-A ruling — with the computation basis disclosed; no uncited external numbers. **Cohort-prior transparency (Gemini pressure-test patch):** the raw capital+age cohort baseline renders ALONGSIDE the model's probability, so the model's efficiency adjustment (how far performance moves a player off his cohort prior) is explicitly visible — the model never presents as an oracle where a table would do. No weekly matchup/points framing anywhere near the QB view (start/sit bleed is the #1 mislead risk); the label's basis disclosed verbatim wherever a survival number renders (games ≥ 8 + snap_share ≥ 0.50 role-occupancy/availability rule, incl. the `games_only` fallback, absent-row conflation, and injury conflation); `decision_supported=false` recursively; all banned-language gates apply. Nothing in this spec constructs a Tier-2/decision pathway.

## 9. Falsification seeds (for the spec review now and the eventual REDs)

1. Regime-change shock: coach/GM fired off-season → survival estimate may move ONLY via features that legitimately encode it; verify no hand-tuned event nudge exists (the model is the anchor).
2. Dead-cap/commitment cliff: carried as qualitative caveat unless a contract data source is ratified (§10) — verify no fabricated contract feature.
3. Day-3 abstention gate: a round-4 rookie QB yields NO model curve — prior + badge only.
4. Backup cap: 4-great-games backup (games_t<8, no qualifying T−1 row) → NO candidate probability emitted at all (abstention-before-scoring); only the `small_sample_qb` caveat + prior disclosure render.
5. Rushing-age discount: dual-threat age-28+ declines faster than pocket peer IF the fitted interaction says so — assert the feature exists and no hardcoded cliff was added (aging-curve ruling).
6. 2024 hole: regenerated labels cover feature-2023 H1 (impossible from the committed frame alone); assert regeneration path, not frame self-join.
7. Absent-row conflation: a QB who retired at T+1 and one who fell to QB30 both label negative — assert the disclosure exists on the label definition wherever surfaced.
8. Label boundary: games exactly 8 = positive-eligible, 7 = negative; **snap_share exactly 0.50 = positive, 0.499 = negative, and a `>= 50` (percent-scale) misread — which produces zero positives everywhere — FAILS the RED (Codex F4)**; snap-share-missing row labels via the pinned `games_only` fallback with `label_basis` disclosed; games_only share per season beyond the 5% tolerance fails/discloses (Codex F6).
8b. Injury-conflation annex (Claude F3/Codex F7): the validation report buckets established-prior-starters-failing-only-via-games<8 as label noise; the label name/disclosure says role-occupancy/availability, never pure "team commitment".
8c. Structural-fold honesty (Claude F1/Codex F5): eligibility computed against per-horizon STRUCTURAL fold counts (H1=4/H2=4/H3=3); an H1 non-promotion under current class balance is a supported report outcome.
9. Lag imputation: a first-year-eligible QB (lags missing, median-imputed) must not silently read as a seasoned mid-tier veteran without the availability flags carrying through.
10. Prior (fork-A, RULED): raw `pick`/`round`/`draft_year` in the candidate matrix FAILS; `draft_capital_prior` present only for position=QB + `nfl_year_at_feature` 1–3 + candidate head; zeroed/absent year 4+; no other position's contract accepts it; `nfl_year_at_feature` absent from `feature_cols`; prior mapping fit train-fold-only (or fixed pre-registered rule).
11. Label-table data contract (Codex R1 #9): duplicate (player_id, season, horizon) rows FAIL; non-bool label FAILS; missing horizon column FAILS; malformed season type FAILS; unknown player_id rows quarantined, never silently scored; duplicate source rows for one QB-season deterministically rejected.
12. Baseline honesty: a promotion case whose Brier baseline was computed from test-fold prevalence is REJECTED (train-fold prevalence only).
