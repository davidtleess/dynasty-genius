# 2026 Roster Vacuum Management — The Productive Struggle Operating Manual

## 1. Purpose

The Great Liquidation creates a deliberate points vacuum on the 2026 roster: ~30+ projected PPG of veteran production removed (Taylor 20+, Adams 14, Hill 8). Filling those spots is itself a strategic decision — *what we put in those slots determines whether our own 2027 1st lands in Tier 1 (1.01–1.03) or drifts into Tier 2 (1.04–1.07)*.

This document defines the operating policy for those vacated spots during the 2026 season.

The strategy's success depends as much on **our own pick landing in Tier 1** as on extracting Tier-1 picks from leaguemates. We are not just hunters — we are also a hunted asset. Per [2027_target_differentiation.md §3.3](2027_target_differentiation.md), our own Roster Fragility Index is a meaningful input.

---

## 2. The Tank-Coherence Principle

**Every roster decision in 2026 should be evaluated against:** *Does this raise or lower the probability that our own 2027 1st lands in Tier 1?*

This is the only question that matters for vacuum-fill decisions. Points scored, weekly entertainment, and "looking competitive" are explicitly secondary — they are *vanity metrics* under the strategy's mission.

**Anti-cheating note:** there is no "polite tanking." Either we are tanking (and our roster decisions reflect it) or we aren't. Half-measures cost us picks. This is not a moral judgment about leaguemates' perceptions; it is a strategic clarity statement.

## 3. PM Question — Stash vs. Cycle & Flip

### Direct answer: **Hybrid, weighted heavily toward Option A (Stash).**

The math is asymmetric. Below is the case in detail.

### 3.1 Option A — The Stash Strategy

**Mechanism:** Hold high-upside backups, developmental rookies, and 2nd/3rd-year lottery tickets in vacated slots. Zero PPG contribution by design.

**E27 implications:**
- Each vacated slot held at ~0 PPG keeps tank coherence intact
- A breakout stash (e.g., a 2nd-year RB inheriting a starting role mid-season) becomes a free trade asset — typical mid-season breakouts return 0.20–0.40 E27 if flipped at deadline peak
- Probability of at least one stash hitting in 6+ slots: empirically ~30–40% per dynasty literature

**Cost:** zero direct cost. Indirect cost is opportunity (slot occupied by a non-producer that could have been a known producer).

### 3.2 Option B — The Cycle & Flip Strategy

**Mechanism:** Sign low-cost veterans, extract production, flip at deadline for 2027 3rds.

**E27 math:**
- Each 2027 3rd ≈ **0.10 E27**
- Realistic cycle: 2–3 successful flips per season → 0.20–0.30 E27 generated
- 5–8 PPG added to weekly total → translates to ~0.5–0.8 added wins over a 17-game season
- Each additional win drops draft order by ~1.0–1.5 spots; movement from 1.01 to 1.04 is **−0.50 E27** loss
- **Net: if Cycle & Flip generates +0.30 E27 in 3rds and costs −0.50 E27 in tank position, the strategy is net negative**

### 3.3 The Hybrid (Recommended)

| Roster Slot Class | 2026 Policy | Rationale |
|---|---|---|
| WR3/WR4 spots | **Stash.** Hold 2nd/3rd-year breakout candidates. | WR breakouts are the highest-hit-rate stash class. |
| RB3/RB4 spots | **Stash.** Hold backup RBs on contender offenses (handcuff-style stashes). | RB1 injuries create instant trade assets. Dynasty literature on this is robust. |
| QB2 (in SF) | **Stash.** Hold a developmental QB (rookie or 2nd-year). | The 2027 strategy is QB-heavy on acquisition. Stash signals coherence. |
| TE2 | **Stash or empty.** | TE has lowest stash hit rate; do not waste FAAB. |
| **Flex slot (1 max)** | **Conditional Cycle & Flip.** | One slot of polite competitiveness preserves trade-flow legitimacy without materially threatening tank. |

**Total expected vacuum effect:** ~3–5 PPG added to the weekly total (vs. ~30 PPG removed by liquidation). Net team PPG declines by ~25 — sufficient to lock Tier 1 finish if our underlying roster is already weakened.

### 3.4 Where Pure-Stash (Pure A) Is Better Than the Hybrid

If by mid-October our actual record is 1–5 or worse, drop the Cycle & Flip flex experiment and go full Stash. The tank is already locked; any added PPG is wasted optimization at the cost of trade-asset depth.

### 3.5 Where Cycle & Flip Becomes the Right Choice (Pure B)

If by mid-October our actual record is 4–2 or better despite the liquidations, the tank has *failed* — the underlying roster is too strong. In that scenario, **the strategy itself needs re-evaluation** (the 2027 Pick Accumulation thesis assumes a successful tank). Cycle & Flip becomes a *consolation* path: extract whatever 2027 3rds are available and revisit the strategy at season end. This is a failure mode, not a success mode.

## 4. Stash Selection Heuristics

Stashes are not random. Use these filters in priority order:

1. **Year 2/3 RBs on offenses with aging RB1s.** Highest mid-season trade-asset conversion rate. Examples of the archetype: backup RB2 on a contender, RB1 over 28.
2. **Year 2 WRs who finished as WR4/WR5 of their team but had elite athletic profiles or top-50 draft capital.** Breakout candidate pool.
3. **Year 2 QBs (in SF only) on teams where the starter has injury history or is over 33.** Insurance against the 2027 strategy's QB acquisition path *and* a potential mid-2026 breakout asset.
4. **Avoid:** TE3+ stashes (poor hit rate), RB committee parts on rebuilding teams (no path to RB1 even on injury), pure athleticism plays without draft capital (low conversion).

**Stash count target:** 4–6 of the vacated slots filled with stashes meeting the above criteria. Above 6 risks becoming "warehouse mode" with no trade-asset clarity.

## 5. Counter-Argument (Rule 5 — Mandatory)

Three reasons this vacuum-management policy could be wrong:

1. **The 5–8 PPG impact of Cycle & Flip on tank odds is *empirical estimate*, not measured fact.** League PPG variance is wide; a "low-cost veteran plug" might add 2 PPG or 12 PPG depending on landing spot. The math in §3.2 uses a midpoint that may overestimate the tank-cost or underestimate the trade-asset gain. **Mitigation:** revisit the policy at the bye week with realized PPG data.

2. **The Stash hit-rate assumption (~30–40%) is informal.** Without an internal backtest of dynasty stash outcomes, this is conventional wisdom — not Engine A or Engine B output. **Mitigation:** flag stash decisions with the `qual_dominant_override` mechanism (per [trade_execution_logic.md §5](trade_execution_logic.md)) so we can backtest at season-end.

3. **League integrity friction.** Hard-tanking via Stash can trigger commissioner review or chat-room hostility in some leagues. The strategy ignores this cost as out-of-scope, but in practice it can cost trade-flow access in subsequent years. **Mitigation:** the Hybrid (one Cycle-and-Flip flex slot) is partly designed to absorb this friction by maintaining visible trade activity. Pure Stash mode raises league-integrity risk meaningfully.
